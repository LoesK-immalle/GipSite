Hallo en welkom op mijn gipwebsite. Mijn naam is Loes Kenis, ik ben 17 jaar en kom uit Sint-Lenaarts. Ik studeer IT en Netwerken, en zit in mijn laatste jaar. In mijn vrije tijd experimenteer ik graag met games maken. Als ik niet achter mijn computer zit, kan je me in de keuken terugvinden. Ik maak taarten, cupcakes, tiramisu en nog veel andere dingen.

Ik zou later graag iets doen wat ik graag doe. Ook wil ik dingen maken die mensen blij maken. Van een van mijn hobby’s zou ik graag mijn beroep maken, het liefst nog van alle twee.

Op deze site vind je mijn geïntegreerde proef. Onder “stage” staat het dagboek dat ik bijhield op mijn observatiestage, een casestudy over Autotask (een service die ze in mijn stagebedrijf gebruikten), een fotoreportage en een analyse van de huisstijl. Op deze pagina staat verder nog een video waarin ik uitleg wat ITN inhoudt en waarom ik deze richting gekozen heb.

Why did I choose this line of study?

My name is Loes, I’m 18 years old and I’m a student at Immaculata. I came a long way and tried a lot of things, but finally I found the department that fits me like a glove. I study IT and Networks.

The things I learned are now helping me create things that others like to use. We learned how to assemble and disassemble computers, program in a variation of languages and manage networks.

It makes us the IT specialists of the family. I’m line one, and if I can’t do it we call 911: my uncle.

In my free time I play some games, but I take more pleasure in creating my own using Unity, a tool to make games. It has an easy interface and the results look professional. With the things I learn during software development at school I write the scripts and with my creativity I create the characters and background etc. This is what I want to proceed in. I would love to create things that make people happy.

During System and Network Management we learn about hardware, operating systems, networks and a lot of other things. It helped me build a new computer, and our class gets to help the school’s IT coordinator sometimes.

We get to do lots of projects. We got to help our school’s fashion department with their haute couture clothes and programmed the lights they sewed into them. This year we get to do all kinds of fun stuff like an internship where we get to see how IT departments in companies or actual IT companies work.

Although our IT courses are very important, the others matter too. We have three hours of all languages: French, English and Dutch. The four hours of mathematics are also very important. Other courses like History, Religion and Aesthetics are less crucial but still intriguing.

I hope that in the following years lots of people choose this department. It would be wonderful if girls continue to study IT, because right now it’s a male dominated business but it doesn’t have to be.
